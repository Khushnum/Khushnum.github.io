🐶ADOPT ! DON'T SHOP🐶

Every day dogs are abandoned by their owners for many reasons. They may not be able to look after the dogs for want of money to feed it,or because they do not have the time to take care of it. Large breeds are sometime taken up when they are puppies. Only when the dog grows up does the owner realise the dog's need for space and freedom.

The sad truth is that not everyone loves animals. Ask any animal welfare officer about the animals who are found bruised, bloodied and emaciated; the litters of puppies who are rescued from taped-up boxes alongside highways or from sealed plastic garbage bags thrown into lakes and rivers; and the animals who are abandoned because they " bark too much " or are aging or because the family is moving.

There are many Rescue Associations doing wonderful work in rescuing anything from mixed breeds to highly pedigreed dogs.

  • What can we do to help?

    • Adopt from animal shelters or from the street – NEVER buy from a pet shop. And don’t forget about adult animals, who are often overlooked by people who are looking for a puppy.

  • Top dog breeds for college students in the Indian climate

    • Dog breeds that college students wish for are those which require less time on proper caring . If you are a college student, you may have neither time nor money. Getting a dog is a serious commitment, so if you have made the decision to get a dog, make sure you are ready for this commitment. Being a college going student, you will probably want a small or medium sized dog which requires less maintenance and has low energy level. There are various dog breeds that are suitable for student lifestyle. These are suitable to your lifestyle and waiting to become your best friend.

    1. Labrador

      Labrador has a soft, silky undercoat that keeps it warm and dry in cold weather and an outer coat that is hard that helps in repelling water. The coat needs to be brushed and groomed to maintain its shine. It can grow up to 32 inches tall and can weigh between 25-34 kgs. It can be found in black, dark brown, light brown, golden, beige and cream color. It loves to eat and has a strong tendency to put on weight; therefore it requires plenty of exercise. It’s a very friendly, playful, loving, caring and sensitive dog. The reason why most people prefer to keep it as a pet is that it’s child friendly.

      [Stay informed-How to take care of labrador dogs?]

    2. Pug

      They are one of the endangered dog breeds and relatively rare in India, but still a favorite with a lot of dog lovers. They are tiny in size, up to 15 inches in height and weight around 6/8 kilograms. They have wrinkly faces and bulging dark eyes. They have a tendency to put on weight so their diet needs to be monitored. Hot and humid weather can give them heatstroke as they have a short muzzle. So care should be taken to keep them cool during summers. They are ideal to keep in small apartments with lesser space.

      [Stay informed-How to take care of pug dogs?]

    3. Dashchund

      Dachshund has three varieties- the Shorthaired, the wire-haired and the long-haired. And it has two sizes- standard and miniature. It has a short and long stretched body shape with an average height of 15 inches and weight around 6.5-11.5 kgs. It’s found in red, black, brown, chocolate and tan colors and lives up to 12-15 years. It requires very less exercise and less food, but has a tendency to put on weight. It’s a funny, playful, intelligent, active and affectionate dog. It is ideal for smaller homes because of its tiny size.

      [Stay informed-How to take care of dashchund dogs?]

    4. Pomeranian

      Pomeranian dogs are a toy breed of the Spitz classification and the direct descendants of the German Spitz type. Their abundant textured coat requires regular brushing and grooming. During shedding season they shed a lot of hair. They are small dogs about 1.9 to 3.5 kgs and 5 to 11 inches in height. They don’t require much exercise because they are active by nature. Very playful and enthusiastic, especially in the company of children. Because of their active nature, they are very good watch dogs. They are low maintenance dogs.
